Gado Gado - Peanut Sauce

  1. Finely cut the shallot and garlic

  2. Fry the shallot and garlic in a pan with the oil until ready

  3. Add the peanut butter and coconut milk. Make sure it gets hot, but don't let it boil. Keep stirring

  4. Keep the sauce cooking until it gets thicker

  5. Once the sauce is thick, take it off the heat and add the ketjap manis and sambal

Note: If the sauce is too thick, a small amount of water can be added to make it thinner

  • 1 shallot (optional)

  • 1 clove garlic

  • 1 tbsp olive oil

  • 2 large tbsp peanut butter (with pieces)

  • 2 tbsp Ketjap manis

  • 200 ml coconut milk

  • 2 tsp sambal

Gado Gado - Veges

  1. Cook the tempeh in a pan with a bit of oil to heat it up

  2. Cook the rice

  3. Cut the carrot and cabbage into strips, cut off the ends of the green beans, and cut the green beans in half

  4. In a pot of boiling water with salt, blanche the veges until ready to eat. Begin by adding the carrot and green beats (3 minutes in total), and after 90 seconds add the cabbage and bean sprouts (1.5minutes total)

  5. After blanching the veges, rinse them under the cold water to stop them cooking further

  6. Put the veges, rice and tempeh onto a plate or bowl. Pour over the peanut sauce, and garnish with the fried onion pieces.

Optional - eggs:

  1. Hard boil the eggs in 7-9 minutes with boiling water

  2. Cool the eggs with cold water and peal the eggs

  3. Cut into quarters before adding to the top of the Gado Gado

  • 100 gram tempeh

  • 1/2 large carrot

  • 130 gram green beans

  • half of a chopped cabbage

  • 100 gram bean sprouts

  • fried onion pieces

  • 1 cup long grain white rice

Optional:

  • 2 eggs
